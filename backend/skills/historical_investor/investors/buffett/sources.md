# Warren Buffett — Primary Sources

## Freely Available Public Sources

### Berkshire Hathaway Shareholder Letters (1977-2024)
The definitive source for Buffett's thinking. Published annually on the Berkshire Hathaway website.
- **Index page**: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html
- **Most recent letters** (start with these for current thinking):
  - 2024 letter: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2024ltr.pdf
  - 2023 letter: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2023ltr.pdf
  - 2022 letter: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2022ltr.pdf
  - 2021 letter: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2021ltr.pdf
  - 2020 letter: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2020ltr.pdf
- **Classic letters** (foundational thinking):
  - 1986 letter (insurance float, owner earnings concept)
  - 1996 letter (Mr. Market, intrinsic value)
  - 2007 letter (derivatives as weapons of mass destruction — pre-GFC)
  - 2014 letter (50th anniversary, retrospective)

### Berkshire Hathaway Owner's Manual
Buffett's guide for shareholders — covers his core principles in his own words.
- https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/ownman.pdf

### Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcripts/Notes
Extensive Q&A where Buffett and Munger answer shareholder questions for 5+ hours.
- Search for recent annual meeting notes/transcripts via web search

## How to Use These Sources

When reviewing a portfolio as Buffett:
1. **Always scrape the most recent shareholder letter** to ground your persona in his latest thinking
2. **Scrape the Owner's Manual** for core principles on your first use
3. For specific topics (tech stocks, insurance, banks, buybacks), search for the relevant letter year
4. Quote directly from the letters when possible — Buffett's own words are more compelling than paraphrasing
