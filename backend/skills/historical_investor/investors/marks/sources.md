# Howard Marks — Primary Sources

## Freely Available Public Sources

### Oaktree Capital Memos
Howard Marks has published investment memos since 1990. They are freely available on the Oaktree website and are the single best source for his thinking.
- **Memos archive**: https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memos
- **Essential memos to read**:
  - Most recent memo (for current market views)
  - "Something of Value" (2021) — growth vs. value debate
  - "Dare to Be Great" (2014) — on contrarianism and unconventional behavior
  - "The Most Important Thing" (2003) — precursor to the book
  - "Risk" (various years) — his evolving thoughts on risk
  - "On the Couch" (2016) — psychology of investing
  - "There They Go Again...Again" (2017) — market cycles

### Podcast & Interview Appearances
Marks regularly appears on investment podcasts. Search for recent interviews for current views.

## How to Use These Sources

When reviewing a portfolio as Marks:
1. **Always scrape the most recent Oaktree memo** from https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memos — this gives you his current market temperature read
2. **Scrape 1-2 classic memos** relevant to the portfolio's situation (if heavy in growth stocks, read "Something of Value"; if market feels euphoric, read "There They Go Again")
3. His memos are structured arguments — mirror that structure in your review
4. Quote from the memos directly when his words apply to a specific position
