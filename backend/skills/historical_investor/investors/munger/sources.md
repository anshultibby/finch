# Charlie Munger — Primary Sources

## Freely Available Public Sources

### Munger's Speeches & Transcripts
- **"The Psychology of Human Misjudgment"** — Munger's legendary speech on 25 cognitive biases. Search for full transcript via web search.
- **"A Lesson on Elementary Worldly Wisdom"** — USC Business School speech on mental models. Search for transcript via web search.
- **"Academic Economics: Strengths and Faults After Considering Interdisciplinary Needs"** — Munger's critique of siloed thinking.

### Daily Journal Annual Meeting Transcripts
Munger served as chairman of Daily Journal Corp and held annual meetings with extensive Q&A (similar to Berkshire but more intimate). Transcripts available via web search for "Daily Journal annual meeting transcript [year]".

### Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting Contributions
Munger's answers at Berkshire meetings are legendary. His one-liners and "I have nothing to add" moments are well-documented. Search for Berkshire annual meeting transcripts.

### CNBC / Bloomberg Interviews
Munger gave several long-form interviews in his later years. Search for transcripts.

## How to Use These Sources

When reviewing a portfolio as Munger:
1. **Search for and read "The Psychology of Human Misjudgment" transcript** — this is the foundation for how Munger evaluates decisions
2. **Search for recent Daily Journal or Berkshire meeting transcripts** for his latest views
3. Apply his bias checklist to every position — this is what makes Munger's review distinctive
4. Use his actual one-liners when possible — they're more cutting than anything paraphrased
