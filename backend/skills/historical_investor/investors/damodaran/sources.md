# Aswath Damodaran — Primary Sources

## Freely Available Public Sources

### Musings on Markets Blog
Damodaran's blog where he publishes detailed company valuations, market commentary, and valuation methodology.
- **Blog**: https://aswathdamodaran.blogspot.com/
- He regularly posts full valuations of companies in the news (Tesla, Nvidia, etc.) — these are masterclasses in narrative-to-numbers.

### NYU Stern Course Materials
Damodaran makes ALL his teaching materials freely available:
- **Main data page**: https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/
- **Valuation course materials**: Lecture slides, spreadsheets, datasets
- **Equity Risk Premium updates**: Monthly updates with current market-implied ERP
- **Valuation spreadsheets**: Template DCF models for different company types

### YouTube Lectures
- Damodaran's full MBA valuation courses are on YouTube (hundreds of hours). Search for "Damodaran valuation course YouTube."
- Short topic videos on specific valuation issues.

### Published Datasets
- Country risk premiums, industry averages (margins, ROIC, beta), cost of capital by sector
- https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/New_Home_Page/datafile.htm
- These are invaluable for benchmarking portfolio positions

## How to Use These Sources

When reviewing a portfolio as Damodaran:
1. **Scrape his most recent blog post** from https://aswathdamodaran.blogspot.com/ for current market thinking
2. **Search for any Damodaran valuation of specific companies** in the portfolio — he may have published one
3. **Reference his data page** for industry benchmarks (cost of capital, margins, ROIC by sector)
4. The key is narrative-to-numbers: for each position, articulate the story, then check if the price implies a reasonable story
5. Always compute or reference the growth-value nexus: ROIC vs WACC determines whether growth helps or hurts
